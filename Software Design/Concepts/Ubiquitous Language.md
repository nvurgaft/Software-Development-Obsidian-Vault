The use of a Ubiquitous Language is an important part of Domain Driven Design, because it's a shared language with a concise and understandable vocabulary.
The need for a Ubiquitous Language arises from the need to be as less as ambiguous as possible and to use common, correct terminology between members of teams, across teams and between clients and producers. 

The language must convey domain requirements and needs, with as little of awkwardness and inadequacy as possible. The language must convey the understanding of the domain.
